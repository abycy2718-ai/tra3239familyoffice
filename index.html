香港家族办公室发展历程

从初步探索到全球领先：香港构建亚洲顶级家族办公室生态圈的战略路径

Family offices emerged in 19th-century Europe, with the Rockefeller family's 1838 establishment marking the first formal single-family office (SFO) to manage their burgeoning oil empire. By the 1980s, U.S. deregulation fueled explosive growth, creating thousands of offices handling investments, philanthropy, and succession. Today, 7,000-10,000 family offices manage US$5.9 trillion globally, with Asia's sector surging due to rapid wealth creation—particularly in China, where 3.16 million high-net-worth individuals (HNWI) drive 10% annual growth in investable assets.

Hong Kong's family office landscape began informally in the pre-2010s era, leveraging its status as an international financial center. Wealthy families from Mainland China and Southeast Asia used private banks and offshore structures for wealth preservation, attracted by unrestricted capital flows and the common law system—unique in China. However, without dedicated policies, operations remained fragmented, often routed through Singapore or traditional banking channels.

家族办公室的起源可追溯至19世纪的欧洲。1838年，洛克菲勒家族成立了首个正式的单一家族办公室（SFO），用于管理其日益壮大的石油财富。到20世纪80年代，美国金融监管放宽推动行业快速发展，数千家办公室开始专注于投资管理、慈善事业及财富传承。目前，全球约有7,000至10,000个家族办公室，管理资产规模约达5.9万亿美元。亚洲市场因财富快速积累而蓬勃发展——特别是在中国，316万高净值人士推动可投资资产以每年10%的速度增长。

香港的家族办公室生态在2010年前处于初步形成阶段，依托其国际金融中心的优势。来自中国大陆及东南亚的富裕家族通过私人银行和离岸架构进行财富管理，看重资本自由流动及香港作为中国境内唯一普通法体系的独特地位。当时由于缺乏针对性政策，市场运作较为分散，部分业务通过新加坡或传统银行渠道进行。

2010年代前：奠定基础

阶段1：奠定基础

Hong Kong's financial infrastructure naturally drew wealthy families from Mainland China and Southeast Asia. Operations were informal, primarily through private banks and offshore entities. The city's role as the world's largest offshore RMB hub and free port status facilitated discreet wealth management, but lacked regulatory clarity or dedicated incentives. Assets under management (AUM) grew steadily, laying groundwork for formal development amid Asia's wealth boom.

香港凭借其金融基础设施，自然吸引了来自中国大陆及东南亚的富裕家族。早期运作以非正式形式为主，主要通过私人银行及离岸实体进行。作为全球最大的离岸人民币枢纽和自由港，香港为私密财富管理提供了便利，但缺乏清晰的监管框架和专项政策支持。资产管理规模（AUM）的稳步增长，为后续在亚洲财富热潮中的正式发展奠定了基础。

2019-2022：政策成型

阶段2：战略布局

The Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) identified family offices as a strategic growth sector amid regional competition from Singapore. InvestHK launched the "Family Office Hub" campaign, positioning Hong Kong as Asia's wealth management center. Key developments included the 2020 introduction of tax concessions for funds and the 2022 Policy Address targeting 200+ family office setups by 2025. The FamilyOfficeHK team was established in June 2021, handling 46 inquiries that year and building a service provider network for one-stop support.

香港金融管理局（HKMA）将家族办公室确定为战略性增长领域，以应对来自新加坡的区域竞争。投资推广署推出「家族办公室枢纽」计划，将香港定位为亚洲财富管理中心。关键进展包括2020年推出的基金税务优惠及2022年《施政报告》中设定的目标——到2025年吸引超过200间家族办公室。FamilyOfficeHK团队于2021年6月成立，当年即处理了46宗查询，并建立了服务提供商网络，提供一站式支持。

2022至今：全面激励

阶段3：生态建设

The landmark March 2023 Policy Statement outlined eight measures to build a competitive ecosystem, including the Inland Revenue (Amendment) Ordinance 2023 for 0% profits tax on qualifying FIHVs (HK$240M+ AUM threshold). The New Capital Investment Entrant Scheme (CIES) relaunched in 2024 with a HK$30M investment threshold, attracting 340+ applications. InvestHK assisted 95 setups in 2024 alone (up from 26 in 2023), exceeding the 200-office target early. The Hong Kong Academy for Wealth Legacy (est. 2023) trained 3,100+ professionals, while SFC guidance clarified licensing exemptions for SFOs. Private wealth AUM from family offices reached HK$1.55 trillion by end-2024, a 76% rise since 2017.

2023年3月发布的《政策宣言》概述了八项措施，以构建具有竞争力的生态系统，其中包括《2023年税务（修订）条例》，为合资格的家族投资控股实体（FIHV）提供0%利得税（资产管理规模门槛为2.4亿港元以上）。新资本投资者入境计划（CIES）于2024年重启，投资门槛为3,000万港元，已吸引超过340宗申请。投资推广署在2024年协助设立了95间办公室（2023年为26间），提前超越了200间的目标。香港财富传承学院（2023年成立）已培训超过3,100名专业人士，而证监会（SFC）的指引也明确了单一家族办公室（SFO）的牌照豁免。截至2024年底，家族办公室管理的私人财富AUM达到1.55万亿港元，自2017年以来增长76%。

2024-2030：全球引领

阶段4：创新发展

The Hong Kong Family Office Nexus launched in October 2024 with Bloomberg and FSTB, featuring a digital knowledge hub (4,000+ views) and The Family Office Playbook (October 2025 release)—a comprehensive guide on regulatory navigation, asset strategies, succession, and operations. The 2025-26 Budget expands tax exemptions to carbon credits, digital assets, and carried interest, targeting 220 more offices by 2028. Greater Bay Area integration enables seamless RMB flows, while green finance and ESG initiatives align with global trends (92% Chinese HNWI in collectibles). With 2,700+ SFOs projected to reach 3,000+, Hong Kong aims to surpass Switzerland as the world's top cross-border wealth center by 2027, managing HK$35T+ AUM.

香港家办汇于2024年10月与彭博及财经事务及库务局合作推出，设有数字知识中心（浏览量超过4,000次）及《家族办公室指南》（预计2025年10月发布）——该指南全面涵盖监管导航、资产策略、传承规划及运营管理。2025-26年度财政预算案将税务豁免范围扩大至碳排放权、数字资产及附带权益，目标是在2028年前吸引220间新办公室。大湾区一体化实现了人民币的无缝流动，绿色金融及ESG倡议与全球趋势相契合（92%的中国高净值人士持有收藏品）。预计单一家族办公室（SFO）数量将从2,700间增至3,000间，香港目标在2027年前超越瑞士，成为全球顶尖的跨境财富中心，管理超过35万亿港元的资产管理规模。

Key Milestones & Future Outlook

Hong Kong's evolution reflects deliberate policy alignment with global trends. From 46 inquiries in 2021 to 648 in 2024, InvestHK's efforts have created a virtuous cycle: 200+ assisted offices bring HK$1.55T in assets, spurring professional services, talent development, and innovation. Challenges like SFC licensing ambiguities are being resolved through 2023's Quick Reference Guide, exempting pure SFOs from Type 9 regulated activities.

Looking ahead, the 2025 Wealth for Good Summit ("Hong Kong of the world, for the world") and Qianhai cross-border measures will enhance connectivity. With China's HNWI base expanding and HK's low US$10M SFO threshold (vs. US$100M in the U.S.), the city is poised for 7.6% annual wealth growth, becoming the ultimate East-West nexus for multi-generational legacies.

Hong Kong has evolved into Asia's premier family office destination, managing HK$31 trillion in assets under management (AUM) as of 2023, with 64% sourced from international investors. This positions the city as the continent's largest hedge fund and cross-border wealth center, second only to Switzerland globally. The private banking and wealth management sector saw family office and trust AUM surge 76% to HK$1.45 trillion from 2017-2023—far outpacing the overall industry's 29% growth—driven by policy support and proximity to China's 3.16 million HNWI (10% annual asset growth).

Under "One Country, Two Systems," Hong Kong offers independent fiscal and monetary policies, unrestricted capital flows, and a common law framework—unique in China—for robust legal certainty. As the world's largest offshore RMB hub, it facilitates seamless cross-border transactions, while its strategic location provides 5-hour access to half the global population. The low US$10 million SFO threshold (vs. US$100 million in the U.S.) and HK$240 million tax-exempt AUM entry make it accessible yet sophisticated.

香港已发展成为亚洲首选的家族办公室目的地，2023年管理的资产管理规模（AUM）达31万亿港元，其中64%来自国际投资者。这使得香港成为亚洲最大的对冲基金及跨境财富中心，全球仅次于瑞士。私人银行及财富管理领域中，家族办公室及信托的AUM在2017-2023年间增长76%至1.45万亿港元——远超行业整体29%的增长率——这得益于政策支持及邻近中国316万高净值人士（其资产年均增长10%）的推动。

在「一国两制」框架下，香港拥有独立的财政及货币政策、资本自由流动以及中国唯一的普通法体系，确保了法律确定性。作为全球最大的离岸人民币枢纽，香港促进了无缝的跨境交易，其战略位置可在5小时内抵达全球一半人口。较低的1,000万美元单一家族办公室门槛（美国为1亿美元）以及2.4亿港元税务豁免AUM的入门要求，使其既易于达到又具备精致服务。

了解家族办公室结构

探索香港支持环境下的单一及多家族模式

Family offices primarily operate as single-family offices (SFOs) or multi-family offices (MFOs), each tailored to different wealth profiles. SFOs serve one ultra-high-net-worth family exclusively, providing maximum customization for complex needs like bespoke investment strategies and intergenerational governance. With a typical US$100 million AUM minimum (U.S. SEC standard), Hong Kong's regime requires HK$2 million annual operating expenditure and two full-time employees for tax benefits, ensuring dedicated service while maintaining privacy.

MFOs pool resources for multiple unrelated families, reducing costs through shared expertise and networks—ideal for emerging UHNWIs with combined AUM often exceeding US$20 million. This model fosters cross-industry collaboration and access to institutional-grade opportunities but may limit personalization. In Hong Kong, MFOs can leverage the unified fund exemption if SFC-licensed, benefiting from streamlined high-net-worth investor assessments. Both types thrive in HK's ecosystem, with 2,700+ SFOs (over half managing US$50M+) and growing MFO presence amid 12,615 local UHNWIs.

家族办公室主要分为单一家族办公室（SFO）和多家族办公室（MFO），分别适合不同的财富状况。SFO专为单个超高净值家族服务，提供高度定制化，应对复杂需求，如量身定制的投资策略及跨代治理。通常最低资产管理规模（AUM）为1亿美元（美国SEC标准），香港制度要求年运营开支200万港元及两名全职员工方可获得税务优惠，在确保专属服务的同时维护隐私。

MFO汇集多个无关家族的资源，通过共享专业知识和网络来降低成本——适合新兴的超高净值人士，合并AUM通常超过2,000万美元。这种模式促进了跨行业合作及机构级机会，但可能限制定制化程度。在香港，若MFO获得证监会（SFC）牌照，可享受统一基金豁免，受益于简化的高净值投资者评估。两类办公室在香港生态中均蓬勃发展，现有2,700多间SFO（其中半数管理着5,000万美元以上的资产）以及不断增长的MFO，同时伴随着12,615名本地超高净值人士。
